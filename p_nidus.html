Nidus

Installation · 2022

Multimedia

Interaction Design

Soundscape

ArduinoIDE

Processing

Team Work

From the Latin “nest”, the title of the project is synonymous to natural receptacle — in this interactive installation, where a dance is set between the spectator and the art pieces, sounds of the environment escape human presence. Designed with the Lisbon Botanical Garden in mind, the installation provides an environment for reflection on deforestation in Portugal.

Each of the four plinths has three electronic elements, which enable the soundscape system programmed with distance sensors, in ArduinoIDE and Processing. This system is housed by four archetypes of nests, built in selected woods — namely oak, eucalyptus, maritime pine and stone pine. A digital guide, in a web app format, allowing detection of distance between the viewer and the nests, offering the possibility to decipher where the sounds are fleeing, and how many sound variants exist, making the experience more immersive.
